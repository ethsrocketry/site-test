Build with us

Join ETHS Rocketry

ETHS Rocketry is where motivated students turn curiosity into real engineering. You will design, build, test, and improve systems that have to work in the real world — while learning with a team that takes mentorship, safety, and iteration seriously.

What you will learn by doing

Rocketry fundamentals

Learn stability, propulsion basics, recovery systems, avionics integration, launch operations, and post-flight analysis through real team projects.

Software + technical workflow

Build practical software habits with version control, debugging, data handling, and lightweight automation used in engineering projects.

CAD and hardware design

Model and revise components in Autodesk Inventor and Onshape, then connect digital designs to fabrication and assembly constraints.

Simulation + analysis

Use OpenRocket and CFD-style analysis to estimate performance, compare design options, and make better design decisions before building.

Electronics + embedded systems

Gain hands-on experience with Arduino-style microcontrollers, sensors, wiring, and test plans for reliable data and control loops.

Engineering design philosophy

Practice iterative design, failure analysis, design reviews, and tradeoff thinking so your work improves every cycle.

Academic and STEM growth

  • Apply math and physics to real systems: force balance, drag, stability margins, trajectories, and system-level constraints.
  • Strengthen core STEM communication skills through documentation, presentations, and design justification.
  • Work in a collaborative engineering environment where planning, testing, and iteration matter as much as raw ideas.

Preparation for collegiate rocketry

The ETHS Rocketry workflow mirrors what collegiate teams look for: systems thinking, technical depth, strong collaboration, and evidence-based decisions. By graduation, members can point to authentic project work in CAD, simulation, electronics, software, and test operations — exactly the kind of experience that helps students contribute quickly on university rocketry teams.
