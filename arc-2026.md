    <div class="section-header">
      <div class="section-tag">System overview</div>
      <h2>PID control fundamentals</h2>
      <p class="section-desc">
        Three corrective forces work together in our feedback loop: tracking error (P), accumulating drift (I), and smoothing oscillation (D).
      </p>
    </div>

    <div class="pid-cards">
      <div class="pid-card p">
        <span class="pid-letter">P</span>
        <div class="pid-name">Proportional</div>
        <p class="pid-desc">
          Directly corrects the error: the farther from target altitude, the more the servo extends the airbrakes.
          Fast but can overshoot.
        </p>
      </div>
      <div class="pid-card i">
        <span class="pid-letter">I</span>
        <div class="pid-name">Integral</div>
        <p class="pid-desc">
          Accumulates error over time to catch slow drift that P and D alone miss.
          Forces corrections when the rocket persistently over- or under-shoots.
        </p>
      </div>
      <div class="pid-card d">
        <span class="pid-letter">D</span>
        <div class="pid-name">Derivative</div>
        <p class="pid-desc">
          Tracks how fast error is changing to smooth servo movement, reduce
          wear, and eliminate oscillation around target.
        </p>
      </div>
    </div>

  <!-- HOW IT WORKS -->
  <section class="section" id="how">
    <div class="section-header">
      <div class="section-tag">Control loop</div>
      <h2>How the system works</h2>
      <p class="section-desc">
        From launch to landing, here's how our PID controller keeps us on target.
      </p>
    </div>

    <div class="pipeline">
      <div class="pipeline-step">
        <div class="step-num">01</div>
        <div class="step-body">
          <h3>Constant tuning via Python SIL sim</h3>
          <p>Before flight, our Python software-in-the-loop simulation runs the physics model with active airbrake dynamics. A binary search script finds optimal Kp, Ki, and Kd constants across dozens of simulated flights.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="pipeline-step">
        <div class="step-num">02</div>
        <div class="step-body">
          <h3>Liftoff & sensor read</h3>
          <p>At launch, the onboard altimeter starts reading pressure data. Altitude and velocity are computed in real time by the C++ flight computer.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="pipeline-step">
        <div class="step-num">03</div>
        <div class="step-body">
          <h3>C++ PID controller runs onboard</h3>
          <p>The controller reads sensor data, computes the P, I, and D terms using the pre-tuned constants, and outputs the target airbrake extension angle to the servo — all in real time.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="pipeline-step">
        <div class="step-num">04</div>
        <div class="step-body">
          <h3>Airbrakes modulate drag</h3>
          <p>The servo extends the airbrakes by the commanded angle, increasing drag to bleed off excess energy. The loop runs continuously until apogee is reached.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="pipeline-step">
        <div class="step-num">05</div>
        <div class="step-body">
          <h3>Recovery & payload check</h3>
          <p>Chutes deploy at apogee. Flight time is logged to confirm the 36–39 second window. The egg is inspected post-landing.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
