<header class="page-header">
    <div class="page-eyebrow">Build with us</div>
    <h1>Join ETHS Rocketry</h1>
    <p>
      ETHS Rocketry is where motivated students turn curiosity into real engineering. You will design, build, test,
      and improve systems that have to work in the real world — while learning with a team that takes mentorship,
      safety, and iteration seriously.
    </p>
  </header>

  <main>
    <section class="section">
      <h2>What you will learn by doing</h2>
      <div class="skills-grid">
        <article class="skill-card">
          <h3>Rocketry fundamentals</h3>
          <p>Learn stability, propulsion basics, recovery systems, avionics integration, launch operations, and post-flight analysis through real team projects.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="skill-card">
          <h3>Software + technical workflow</h3>
          <p>Build practical software habits with version control, debugging, data handling, and lightweight automation used in engineering projects.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="skill-card">
          <h3>CAD and hardware design</h3>
          <p>Model and revise components in Autodesk Inventor and Onshape, then connect digital designs to fabrication and assembly constraints.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="skill-card">
          <h3>Simulation + analysis</h3>
          <p>Use OpenRocket and CFD-style analysis to estimate performance, compare design options, and make better design decisions before building.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="skill-card">
          <h3>Electronics + embedded systems</h3>
          <p>Gain hands-on experience with Arduino-style microcontrollers, sensors, wiring, and test plans for reliable data and control loops.</p>
        </article>
        <article class="skill-card">
          <h3>Engineering design philosophy</h3>
          <p>Practice iterative design, failure analysis, design reviews, and tradeoff thinking so your work improves every cycle.</p>
        </article>
      </div>
    </section>

    <section class="section">
      <h2>Academic and STEM growth</h2>
      <ul>
        <li>Apply math and physics to real systems: force balance, drag, stability margins, trajectories, and system-level constraints.</li>
        <li>Strengthen core STEM communication skills through documentation, presentations, and design justification.</li>
        <li>Work in a collaborative engineering environment where planning, testing, and iteration matter as much as raw ideas.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section class="section">
      <h2>Preparation for collegiate rocketry</h2>
      <p>
        The ETHS Rocketry workflow mirrors what collegiate teams look for: systems thinking, technical depth, strong collaboration,
        and evidence-based decisions. By graduation, members can point to authentic project work in CAD, simulation, electronics,
        software, and test operations — exactly the kind of experience that helps students contribute quickly on university rocketry teams.
      </p>
